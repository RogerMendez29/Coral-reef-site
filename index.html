New Caledonia Barrier Reef

Located in the South Pacific the New Caledonia Barrier Reef is the longest continous reef in the globe. It is approximately 1,500 Kilometers long and is only 30 kilometers away form the shore. this reef holds one of the world's most diverse set of reef structures, along with also having a large diversity of species of fish and coral. It is home to the endangered dugongs and is also nesting stite for sea turtles.

Red Sea Coral Reefs

The Red Sea is a sewater inlet of the indian ocean it is located inbetween Saudi Arabia, egypt and Sudan. it has a large number of shallow shelves which holds a large diversity of corals and fish. this is due to its location and how it echhanges its water mass with the Gulf of Aden,Arabian Sea and the Indian Ocean. for these reasons coral bleaching is virtually non existant here making it a pristine place to dive and sea an incredible diversity of fish and corals.

Rainbow Reef

The Rainbow Reef is located in the south pacific inbetween the islands of Fiji and the Somosomo Strait. It is a famous dive site known for its Great White Wall which is and area of white coral located at depths between 49 and 213 feet.

Raja Ampat tha Reefs, Indonesia

Located in West Papua, Indonesia the Rajat Ampat is home to one of the worlds most biodiverse dive sites. it has approximately 1,400 fish species and about 75% of a coral species are located in these reefs, So if your into corals this is the place to visit. The reefs are in a marine protected area of roughly one million hectares, this area prhibits fishing is mainly why its one of the most pristine places to scuba dive.

The Great Barrier Reef

The Great Barrier Reef is the holy grail of coral reefs it is located of the coast of Australia. It streches over 1,400 miles making it the largest coral reef in the world, it is so large that it can be seen form outer space. The number of species it supports is incredible with over 400 coral species, 1500 species fish, 30 species of cetaceans a large number of those being endangered, six species of sea turtles and 17 species of sea snakes. Due to climate change and it not being completly marine protected a mass number of of coral bleaching events have occured diminishing its number over the last couple of decades.
